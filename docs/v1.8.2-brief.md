# v1.8.2 — pong-deadline alignment with paced-transport budget window

**Release type:** Single-constant tuning release on top of v1.8.1. Bumps `PONG_DEADLINE_SECS` from 15 → 90 to eliminate structural peer-connection drops caused by the interaction of the pong-timeout supervisor with v1.8.0's per-peer 16 MiB/min response budget. No protocol changes, no consensus changes, no other timing constants touched.

**Spec status:** Single round, no prior review iterations needed. Scope is one constant change + test update + this brief.

---

## Background

v1.8.0 introduced per-peer paced transport with `MAX_RESPONSE_BYTES_PER_MIN = 16 MiB` and a 60-s tumbling-reset window enforced at `sync.rs:3641`. When a peer hits its budget mid-cycle, it goes silent for up to 60 s while waiting for the window to roll. The pong-timeout supervisor at `sync.rs:4232–4321` uses:

- `PING_INTERVAL_SECS = 60` — supervisor sends a Ping when inbound `bytes_read` hasn't advanced for 60 s.
- `PONG_DEADLINE_SECS = 15` — after Ping is sent, supervisor tears down the peer with `PeerError::PongTimeout` if no Pong arrives within 15 s of the deadline anchor (max of `ping_sent_at`, `last_read_progress`).

Two reinforcing failure modes against a budget-paced peer:

1. **Budget-silence race.** Peer's 16 MiB/min budget exhausts mid-cycle → peer stops sending → our `last_read_progress` freezes for ~60 s → supervisor fires Ping → 15-s tolerance can race the budget-window boundary even when the peer responds promptly.

2. **Writer head-of-line blocking.** Peer's writer is mid-frame on a large `BlockResponse` over a slow link. `WriterControl::SendPong` is queued with `biased` priority (`peer.rs:645–721`), but the in-progress `write_framed_message` await blocks the writer until the current frame finishes. A ~4 MiB block over a slow downlink can take 20-30 s to drain before Pong reaches the wire. The 15-s deadline fires first.

**Empirical evidence:** v1.8.0 cold-bootstrap on a residential link recorded ~7 peer-connection drops during a 44-min Stage B IBD phase, costing ~7-14 min of total cold-boot wall-clock. Total cold-boot time was 91 min vs. a projected ~70 min absent the churn.

`PeerError::PongTimeout` does **not** record a strike (per the v1.7.2 round-2 spec correction). So the cost of a longer deadline is bounded: a truly-dead-but-app-alive peer lingers in the connected list ~75 s longer before reconnect. No reputation, eviction policy, or protocol invariant depends on the current value.

---

## Invariant

Application-layer pong-timeout detection must tolerate the peer's full 60-s budget-silence window plus mid-frame writer latency on slow links. The pong-deadline must therefore exceed the budget window with comfortable margin.

**Chosen value:** 90 s = 1.5× the 60-s budget window. Aligns with the v1.8.0 paced-transport timeout family:

| Constant | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| `PONG_DEADLINE_SECS` (this release) | 90 | `src/types/mod.rs` |
| `STAGE_A_PROGRESS_TIMEOUT` (v1.8.0) | 120 | `src/network/sync.rs` |
| `TIP_VALIDATION_BATCH_TIMEOUT_SECS` (Fix 2) | 120 | `src/types/mod.rs` |

90 s is tight enough to keep dead-connection detection responsive while comfortably exceeding the 60-s budget window with 50% margin.

## Empirical gate

Re-run cold-bootstrap from fresh empty datadir on a residential link via `seed.exfer.org` with the v1.8.2 binary.

**Targets:**
- Total wall-clock from gate start to `IBD complete at height N`: **≤ 60 min** (vs. 91 min observed on v1.8.0).
- `Stage B run_ibd failed via X: IBD peer disconnected` events during full Stage B: **≤ 2** (vs. ~7 on v1.8.0).
- `record_ip_strike` count attributable to our node: **0** (PongTimeout never strikes; this is a pin, not a change).

If the gate passes, ship. If the gate doesn't show meaningful improvement, the residual disconnects are on a different mechanism — investigate before shipping.

## Rejected alternatives

- **Lengthen `PING_INTERVAL_SECS` (60 → 120):** doesn't help — same 15-s tolerance still bites once Ping fires; just shifts the timer window.
- **TCP-level `SO_KEEPALIVE`:** complementary, not a substitute. Doesn't catch app-alive-but-silent peers, only true TCP-dead connections. Worth considering separately in a future release.
- **Regime-aware deadline (lax pre-confirmation, strict post-confirmation):** mirrors v1.7.2's permissive-transport pattern, but adds complexity for limited benefit. The drops happen equally in pre- and post-confirmation phases — budget-driven silence is structural, not regime-dependent.
- **Outbound-liveness (writer-side `last_write_progress`):** real defense-in-depth that would treat our own outbound activity as evidence the peer is alive. Multi-file change touching `PeerSharedState` + four `write_framed_message` sites + the supervisor anchor formula. Out of scope for v1.8.2's "small parameter-bump" envelope. Filed as a v1.9 design note; revisit if the v1.8.2 empirical gate shows Fix 1 alone is insufficient.
