# v1.7.2 — Cold-bootstrap fix: permissive pre-confirmation bootstrap (generalized)

**Release type:** Policy release on top of v1.7.1. Generalizes v1.7.1's narrowly-targeted pre-confirmation BlockResponse-rate-limit exemption to the **bootstrap-control sync-traffic-quota paths** (Ping, Pong-unsolicited, NewBlock, GetTip, BlockResponse, GetAddr-unsolicited, Addr-out-of-window — 7 sites; explicitly **NOT** NewTx and **NOT** the `_` catch-all unsolicited bucket, which handles post-handshake protocol garbage like repeated Hello/AuthAck), **plus** gates the `SlowPeer` cleanup strike on `ever_confirmed_peer` at the strike-arm classifier. No new constants, no protocol changes, no consensus changes. Policy invariant: **during pre-confirmation bootstrap, soft transport failures do not accumulate durable identity/IP reputation.** Session teardown at the transport layer is not targeted by this release. Protocol-violation strikes (invalid content, HMAC failure, post-handshake protocol garbage) are not relaxed.

**Spec status:** Round 5 — revised after round-4 review.

Round-5 fixes one remaining round-4 issue:

- Round-4's Change A list included the `_` catch-all unsolicited path at `sync.rs:4038`. The reviewer pointed out that this path fires on messages with no legitimate post-handshake reason to appear — specifically `Message::Hello` and `Message::AuthAck` repeated after handshake, plus any future protocol-garbage variants. That is not "bootstrap-control sync traffic"; it's a protocol-violation guard. Exempting it pre-confirmation would let an authenticated colonizing peer spray arbitrary protocol garbage without consequence until first confirmation. **Round-5 removes the `_` catch-all from Change A's gated list.** Remaining 7 gated sync-traffic-quota sites: Ping, Pong-unsolicited, NewBlock (defense-in-depth), GetTip, BlockResponse (v1.7.1 existing), GetAddr-unsolicited, Addr-out-of-window. Added a regression test (`protocol_garbage_catchall_still_fires_pre_confirmation`) to pin the behaviour.

Earlier rounds (preserved for context):

- Round 4: fixed round-3's non-behaviour-preserving "inline the gate at the cap-check" pattern. Correct pattern is v1.7.1's skip-increment shape (no counter debt during bootstrap), applied uniformly. Background-line cleanup to remove the "no teardown" conflict with the narrowed invariant.
- Round 3: SlowPeer narrative narrowed to no-strike-only (teardown still happens at transport layer, outside Change B's reach); NewTx excluded from gated list (application-layer mempool, not bootstrap-control); "idempotent strike" language corrected to "mild pre-existing double-count orthogonal to this spec."
- Round 2: fixed round-1's Change-C-causes-disconnect issue (kept handler-local exemption and generalized) and PongTimeout-mis-added-to-strike-arm.
- Round 1: framed the invariant correctly but had three implementation errors.

**Empirical verification:** Fresh-Mac / residential / unknown-IP gate. Target: ≤ 30 min wall-clock to tip, **zero self-inflicted IP/identity strikes** attributable to our node during the pre-confirmation run. Transport-layer disconnects (TCP close, SlowPeer tear-down) are expected on flaky residential connections and are not part of the ship predicate; what matters is that each disconnect is followed by a successful re-contact instead of by a permanent ban.

## Background (carried from round-1)

v1.4.2 had no Fix 2 and worked. v1.5.0 added Fix 2 as a hard pre-IBD precondition. Every release since has been patching one transport-layer strike source at a time: eviction thrash (v1.6.0), BlockResponse rate-limit (v1.7.1), now SlowPeer and generalized sync-quota. Expert reframe: stop whacking moles, state the invariant: **before `ever_confirmed_peer == true`, transport-layer soft failures do not accumulate durable reputation damage.** Only provable-lie / content-malice classes strike. The transport-level disconnect itself is left alone; what matters is that it does not carry a persistent IP/identity strike that would bar the peer from being re-contacted on the next outbound retry.

## Scope

Two sets of code changes at distinct layers:

### Change A: Generalize handler-local pre-confirmation exemption to all sync-traffic-quota sites

Sync-traffic-quota `return Err(PeerError::RateLimitExceeded)` sites in the reader loop, classified by whether they are sync-traffic-quota (exempt pre-confirmation) or content-wrapper (leave striking):

| Line | Handler | Counter / cap | Category | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3598 | pre-loop | `is_ip_banned` | terminal wrapper (pre-existing ban) | **unchanged** — a peer already banned stays banned |
| 3604 | pre-loop | `is_identity_banned` | terminal wrapper | **unchanged** |
| 3617 | Ping | `ping_count > MAX_PINGS_PER_MIN` | sync-traffic-quota | **gate on `ever_confirmed_peer`** |
| 3627 | Pong unsolicited | `unsolicited_count > MAX_UNSOLICITED_PER_MIN` | sync-traffic-quota | **gate** |
| 3640 | NewBlock | `block_count > MAX_BLOCKS_PER_MIN` (already guarded by `catching_up`) | sync-traffic-quota | **gate as defense-in-depth** (existing `catching_up` exemption is the primary; adding `ever_confirmed_peer` covers the fallback-to-Live pre-confirmation window for symmetry) |
| 3658, 3661 | NewBlock invalid | `record_ip_strike` / `invalid_block_count > MAX_INVALID_BLOCKS_PER_PEER` | content-wrapper (invalid block already struck inline) | **unchanged** — malice evidence is real |
| 3685 | NewTx | `tx_count > MAX_TXS_PER_MIN` | application-layer mempool (NOT bootstrap-control) | **unchanged** — fresh nodes don't need to accept NewTx pre-confirmation, and keeping the cap preserves anti-spam during the most fragile phase |
| 3710, 3778, 3790 | NewTx invalid | DoubleSpend / tx validation failed + inline strike | content-wrapper | **unchanged** |
| 3798 | GetTip | `request_count > MAX_REQUESTS_PER_MIN` | sync-traffic-quota | **gate** |
| 3909 | BlockResponse | `block_count > MAX_BLOCKS_PER_MIN` (v1.7.1 already has `pre_confirmation_bootstrap` inline — skip-increment shape) | sync-traffic-quota | **keep v1.7.1 code unchanged**; the other 6 handlers adopt the same skip-increment shape |
| 3930 | BlockResponse invalid | `record_ip_strike` fired inline | content-wrapper | **unchanged** |
| 3990 | GetAddr unsolicited | `unsolicited_count > MAX_UNSOLICITED_PER_MIN` (fires when peer sends repeated GetAddr) | sync-traffic-quota | **gate** |
| 4008 | Addr unsolicited-window | `unsolicited_count > MAX_UNSOLICITED_PER_MIN` (Addr delivered outside the GetAddr response window — could be a peer pre-emptively sharing its book) | sync-traffic-quota | **gate** |
| 4038 | `_` catch-all unsolicited | `unsolicited_count > MAX_UNSOLICITED_PER_MIN` (fires on messages with no legitimate post-handshake reason to appear — e.g. repeated `Message::Hello`, repeated `Message::AuthAck`) | **protocol-garbage guard, NOT bootstrap-control** | **unchanged** — one of the few disconnect levers against a misbehaving authenticated peer; exempting it would let a colonizing peer spray arbitrary protocol garbage until first confirmation without consequence |

The gated sites each get the same shape — **skip the counter increment AND the cap check during pre-confirmation**, matching v1.7.1's existing BlockResponse semantics exactly:

```rust
// Before (v1.7.1 and earlier, non-BlockResponse sites):
ping_count += 1;
if ping_count > MAX_PINGS_PER_MIN {
    return Err(PeerError::RateLimitExceeded);
}

// After (v1.7.2, all 7 gated sync-traffic-quota sites):
if self.ever_confirmed_peer.load(Ordering::Relaxed) {
    ping_count += 1;
    if ping_count > MAX_PINGS_PER_MIN {
        return Err(PeerError::RateLimitExceeded);
    }
}
// Pre-confirmation: no increment, no check. Crucially, no counter
// debt accumulates during the bootstrap window, so the moment
// ever_confirmed_peer flips to true, a peer that delivered useful
// traffic during bootstrap does not instantly trip the cap on its
// next message.
```

For the BlockResponse site (3909), v1.7.1 already uses this shape with a named local `pre_confirmation_bootstrap`. Leave it as-is (or inline the load for minor consistency — behaviour is identical, unlike the round-3 proposal which would have changed semantics). Round-4 explicitly drops the round-3 "Change C" that would have flattened the existing `if !pre_confirmation_bootstrap { counter += 1; check }` pattern into `counter += 1; if over && confirmed { Err }` — that flattening was not behaviour-preserving.

**Why skip-increment vs gate-at-check matters.** Both shapes produce the same pre-confirmation behaviour (no strike, no teardown). They differ at the *transition* when `ever_confirmed_peer` flips to true:

- **Skip-increment (v1.7.1 / this round):** counter starts at 0 after the flip, peer gets a full post-confirmation minute of budget. No inherited debt.
- **Gate-at-check (round-3 error):** counter accumulated throughout pre-confirmation; at the flip, a noisy-but-useful peer could already be well over the cap and get RateLimitExceeded on its next message. Exactly the peers we want to keep — the ones who were helping us bootstrap — are the ones most likely to be kicked.

The v1.7.1 shape is the correct one; applying it uniformly across the other 6 handlers is what Change A is.

**Why this is necessary (not just strike-arm gating):** the reader returning `Err(RateLimitExceeded)` tears the session down at `sync.rs:4163`, killing any Fix 2 validation in flight on that peer. Suppressing only the strike at `sync.rs:3263` leaves the session still dead. The handler-local exemption prevents the `Err` from being raised in the first place when the peer is honest under pre-confirmation conditions.

### Change B: Strike-arm exempts `SlowPeer` during pre-confirmation

At `sync.rs:3247-3265` and the symmetric arm at `sync.rs:3460-3478`, split the combined arm:

```rust
// Before:
Err(PeerError::HmacFailure)
| Err(PeerError::RateLimitExceeded)
| Err(PeerError::SlowPeer(_)) => {
    warn!(
        "Authenticated peer {} disconnected ({}) — recording IP strike",
        addr, result.as_ref().unwrap_err()
    );
    self.record_ip_strike(addr.ip(), Some(meta.identity));
    // ... additional logic ...
}

// After:
Err(PeerError::HmacFailure) => {
    // Crypto auth failure: always strike pre- or post-confirmation.
    warn!("Authenticated peer {} disconnected (HMAC failure) — recording IP strike", addr);
    self.record_ip_strike(addr.ip(), Some(meta.identity));
    // ... additional logic ...
}
Err(PeerError::RateLimitExceeded) => {
    // With Change A, sync-traffic-quota breaches never reach here
    // during pre-confirmation. Any RateLimitExceeded that DOES reach
    // here is either a content-wrapper exit (inline record_ip_strike
    // already fired — a pre-existing mild double-count via this arm
    // that's orthogonal to v1.7.2) or a terminal ban wrapper (correct
    // response). Policy unchanged.
    warn!("Authenticated peer {} disconnected (rate limit exceeded) — recording IP strike", addr);
    self.record_ip_strike(addr.ip(), Some(meta.identity));
    // ... additional logic ...
}
Err(PeerError::SlowPeer(_)) => {
    // v1.7.2 Change B: transport-layer frame/read timeout. The TCP
    // session is already torn down at peer.rs:1233 / supervisor
    // sync.rs:4163 before we reach this cleanup arm — we can't and
    // don't try to prevent the disconnect itself at this layer.
    // What we DO change is whether the disconnect leaves persistent
    // reputation damage. Pre-confirmation: no strike, so the peer
    // can be re-contacted on the next outbound retry without
    // tripping an IP ban. Post-confirmation: strike as before
    // (we've worked with this peer, silence mid-message is suspect).
    if self.ever_confirmed_peer.load(Ordering::Relaxed) {
        warn!("Authenticated peer {} disconnected ({}) — recording IP strike",
              addr, result.as_ref().unwrap_err());
        self.record_ip_strike(addr.ip(), Some(meta.identity));
        // ... additional logic ...
    } else {
        info!("Authenticated peer {} disconnected ({}) — pre-confirmation, no strike",
              addr, result.as_ref().unwrap_err());
        // session teardown already happened at peer.rs/supervisor; we
        // simply skip the IP-strike step
    }
}
```

**`PongTimeout` is deliberately not modified.** It is produced at `sync.rs:4221` inside the supervisor-layer liveness check and is NOT currently caught by either strike arm. Its existing disposition (session dies, no strike pre- or post-confirmation) is unchanged.

**Scope clarity on SlowPeer.** Round-2 mis-described Change B as achieving "no teardown" for SlowPeer. Round-3 corrects: session teardown on SlowPeer **still happens** at the transport layer; Change B only affects the cleanup strike. This is sufficient for cold-bootstrap because the failure mode we're fixing is **strike → ban cascade**, not individual disconnects. A peer that disconnects once on SlowPeer and can be reconnected 30 seconds later without hitting a ban is fine — Fix 2 gets another attempt. What was breaking cold-bootstrap before v1.7.2 was that each disconnect carried a durable strike, and strikes accumulated until the IP was banned for 600 s, removing the peer permanently from the usable set.

### (Change C dropped in round-4)

Round-3 proposed a Change C to consolidate v1.7.1's named `pre_confirmation_bootstrap` local into the same inline check pattern as the other 7 handlers. That consolidation was described as "stylistic / same behaviour." Round-4 review showed it was not — the proposed inline form would accumulate `block_count` debt during pre-confirmation (which v1.7.1's current code does not), potentially kicking a useful peer on its first post-confirmation BlockResponse. **Change C is dropped.** v1.7.1's BlockResponse exemption stays exactly as-is in code; Change A brings the other 7 handlers up to the same shape.

## Test plan

Unit / integration:

1. **`sync_traffic_quota_does_not_fire_pre_confirmation`** — per-quota, one test each or a parameterized suite. With `ever_confirmed_peer = false`, send 70 pings in 60 s (cap = 10). Assert: reader does not return `RateLimitExceeded`, session stays alive.
2. **`sync_traffic_quota_fires_post_confirmation`** — same send pattern with `ever_confirmed_peer = true`. Assert: `RateLimitExceeded` returned after the cap, strike recorded.
3. **`content_wrapper_strike_still_fires_pre_confirmation`** — peer delivers trivially invalid NewBlock (height=0). Assert: strike recorded inline (existing behaviour), session torn down. Pre-confirmation permissiveness does NOT shield this path.
4. **`hmac_failure_still_strikes_pre_confirmation`** — inject `HmacFailure` with `ever_confirmed_peer = false`. Assert: strike recorded (provable lie supersedes permissiveness).
5. **`slow_peer_does_not_strike_pre_confirmation`** — inject `PeerError::SlowPeer(_)` with `ever_confirmed_peer = false`. Assert: session torn down (expected — transport layer), **no IP strike recorded in `ip_abuse`**. The test explicitly asserts the teardown happens, because that's preserved behaviour, not a regression.
6. **`slow_peer_strikes_post_confirmation`** — same injection with `ever_confirmed_peer = true`. Assert: strike recorded (regression check).
7. **`pong_timeout_unchanged`** — simulate peer not responding to ping. Assert: `PongTimeout` session-tears-down as before, no strike at either state (pre- or post-confirmation).
8. **`protocol_garbage_catchall_still_fires_pre_confirmation`** (round-5 regression) — post-handshake authenticated peer sends 20 repeated `Message::Hello` (or `Message::AuthAck`) with `ever_confirmed_peer = false`. These hit the `_` catch-all arm at `sync.rs:4038` and bump `unsolicited_count`. Assert: after the `MAX_UNSOLICITED_PER_MIN` cap, `RateLimitExceeded` IS returned, session IS torn down, strike IS recorded. Pre-confirmation permissiveness does NOT shield protocol-garbage senders — the catch-all is a protocol-violation guard, not a bootstrap-control quota.
9. **`fresh_node_transport_flake_cascade_does_not_erode_peer_set`** (end-to-end) — fresh node against 5 mock seeds, 2 go silent mid-handshake (SlowPeer), 1 floods BlockResponse past `MAX_BLOCKS_PER_MIN` during orphan-chase (RateLimitExceeded from sync-traffic), 1 is honest fast peer, 1 is honest slow Fix-2-succeeding peer. Assert: 0 strikes, remaining peers usable, Fix 2 eventually confirms on one of the honest peers.

Empirical (release gate):

10. **`coldboot_fresh_mac_residential_reaches_tip_cleanly_v172`** — fresh Mac / residential / clean datadir / `seed.exfer.org` DNS path. Target: ≤ 30 min to tip, **zero self-inflicted IP/identity strikes attributable to our node during the pre-confirmation run**. Transport-layer disconnects (TCP close, SlowPeer frame-read timeout) are expected on flaky residential connections and are NOT part of the ship predicate; what matters is that each disconnect is followed by a successful re-contact / re-handshake on the next outbound retry, with no accumulated strike → ban cascade.

## What this does NOT do

- Does not touch Fix 2's forward-chain validator internals. Validator logic is unchanged from v1.7.0.
- Does not drop Fix 2. Invalid-header strikes still fire.
- Does not relax post-confirmation policy. Once `ever_confirmed_peer == true`, all strike classes fire as before.
- Does not introduce new constants, protocol changes, or consensus changes.
- Does not modify `PongTimeout` behaviour. It remains non-strike pre- and post-confirmation.
- Does not modify content-wrapper strike paths. A peer delivering invalid blocks/txs still gets struck during pre-confirmation — provable lies are always strike-worthy.
